Referencing

Referencing is a way for authors to acknowledge research from other authors on your written piece. Referencing is very important in writing and there are several different styles of referencing:

  • MLA
  • APA
  • Oxford
  • Harvard
  • Chicago

Harvard Style Reference is an author/date method. Sources are cited within the body of your assignment by indicating the name of the author(s) followed by the date of publication. All other details of the publication are given in the list of references or at the end of the bibliography.

Each style has its own rules for properly citing sources and you must remember that references must always be in alphabetical order when creating a reference list, while multiple references by the same author must be in chronological order. Citations form anonymous authors must begin with “Anon” followed by the date e.g. Anon, (2020). If the year of publication is not specified but can be established, it should be provided.
